About me!

Tim Berners Lee is a software engineer who invented the internet (or World Wide Web) in 1989. He is a Professorial Fellow of Computer science at the University of Oxford, and a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). In 2011, he was named a member of the board of trustees of the Ford Foundation. He is the founder and president of the Open Data Insitute and currently an advisor at social network MeWe.

In 2004, Tim Berners-Lee was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his work. He was named in Time Magazine's list of 100 most Imprtant People of the 20th century, and was honored as the "Inventor of the World Wide Web" during the 2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony.

In 1994, Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consoritum (W3C) at the Laboratory of Computer Science (LCS) at the MIT. It was made up of companies that wanted to work together to create Web standards and also improve the Web. In 1995, Microsoft produced the first version of Internet Explorer and this commercial browser created enormous groeth in website and web users.
